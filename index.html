I am a maître de conférences (HDR) in applied mathematics at Université Paris-Saclay (Laboratoire de mathématiques d'Orsay) and a junior member of the Institut Universitaire de France. I defended my HdR in March 2024; before that I was a post-doc with Mathieu Lewin, following a PhD (2016) under Jean-David Benamou and Guillaume Carlier.

My research develops generalized optimal transport models and methods, with applications to mathematical physics, economics and numerical optimization (and sometimes PDEs).

I am the Principal Investigator of the ANR project GOTA, co-PI of the associate team KarMa (ParMa–Kantorovich Initiative, PIMS), and a member of the Inria–Saclay team ParMa. I co-organize the monthly Optimal Transport / PDE / Machine Learning seminar at Orsay.
